Before you visit.

よくあるご質問　—　FAQ
I — Basics

Visiting the temple

参拝について
01 Can non-Buddhists and foreign visitors enter the temple?

Yes — all are welcome during open hours (7:00 — 17:00), regardless of background or belief. No reservation is needed, and there is no admission fee. You are welcome to walk the grounds, sit quietly in the main hall, or simply pass through on your way up the hillside.

02 Do I need to pay to enter?

No. Entry to the grounds and the main hall is free. A small offering box (saisen-bako) sits in front of the altar if you wish to give — a coin of any denomination is fine.

03 How long does a visit usually take?

A quiet walk through the grounds takes about 30 minutes. If you would like to sit in the main hall or read the plaques along the way, budget an hour. A full guided tour (by appointment) is roughly 90 minutes.

04 Can I reach the temple on foot from Shin-Kobe Station?

Yes. It is a 15-minute walk — mostly flat, with a short climb at the end. From Sannomiya Station it is about 25 minutes on foot, or 10 minutes by taxi. See the map on the home page for the route.

II — Etiquette

Inside the hall

作法・マナー
05 Is there etiquette I should follow inside the hall?

Nothing strict. Please remove your shoes at the step before the main hall — slippers are provided. Speak softly, keep phones on silent, and feel free to place your palms together (gasshō) before the altar if you wish.

You do not need to chant, bow, or follow any particular form. Sitting quietly is itself a kind of practice.

06 Can I take photographs?

Photography is welcome throughout the grounds. Inside the main hall please refrain from using flash, and avoid photographing worshippers or ceremonies in progress. If a service is underway, it is best to wait until it is over.

07 Is there a dress code?

No formal dress code. Casual clothing is fine; we simply ask that shoulders and knees be reasonably covered, especially inside the main hall. Easily-removed shoes are helpful, as you will take them off before entering.

III — Practical

Accessibility & support

アクセシビリティ・対応
08 Is the temple wheelchair-accessible?

Yes. A continuous, gently climbing ramp leads from the street-level entrance up to the second and third floors, with a lift connecting the ground floor to the upper halls. Visitors with limited mobility are welcome to ring the bell at the lower entrance for assistance.

09 Are there English-speaking guides or materials?

The temple office can usually answer questions in simple English during office hours (9:00 — 17:00). For a fuller tour or to meet the head priest, please email or phone in advance — a few days' notice is ideal. Written materials in English are available on request.

10 Is there a café or a place to sit?

The temple recently opened a small in-house dining room, Sekai, currently by reservation for groups only. Benches sit along the koi garden and beside the main hall — a good spot to rest after the climb.

IV — Practice

Faith & ceremonies

信仰・法要
11 What is Nichiren-shū? How is it different from other Buddhist schools?

Nichiren-shū is one of the major schools of Japanese Buddhism, founded in the 13th century by Nichiren Shōnin. Its practice centres on the Lotus Sūtra and the chanted phrase Namu Myōhō Renge Kyō.

Read more on our About page.

12 Can I attend a ceremony or memorial service?

Most regular services are open to quiet observation — there is no need to register. Monthly observances and major annual ceremonies are also streamed on our YouTube channel.

13 I'd like to request a memorial or prayer service. Is that possible?

Yes — we accept requests for memorial services (hōyō), prayer rites (kitō), and ancestor offerings from anyone, regardless of denomination or nationality. Please contact the office at least two weeks in advance to arrange a time.

14 Can I bring children?

Of course. Children are welcome on the grounds and in the main hall. The koi garden in particular is a favourite — a quiet space to walk and watch the fish.

Still have a question?

If your question isn't answered here, the temple office is the best place to start. We respond in simple English during office hours, and can usually arrange a guided visit with a few days' notice.

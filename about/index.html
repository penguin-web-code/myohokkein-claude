I — The School

What is Nichiren-shū?

One of the major schools of Japanese Buddhism, founded in the 13th century by Nichiren Shōnin. Its practice centres on the Lotus Sūtra and the mantra Namu Myōhō Renge Kyō.

日蓮宗とは

Nichiren-shū holds that the Lotus Sūtra — the teaching the historical Buddha is said to have preserved as his final and most essential — contains the complete truth of Buddhism, and that this teaching is especially intended for the present age.

The school centres practice on three cardinal observances (三大秘法): the Object of Veneration (本門の本尊), the Sacred Title chanted as Namu Myōhō Renge Kyō (本門の題目), and the Sanctuary of ordination (本門の戒壇). Through chanting, study, and daily conduct, practitioners aspire to awaken to the Buddha within themselves and to manifest a Buddha-land in the world as it is.

"Practice and study arise from faith. If you have the strength, speak — even a single phrase, a single line."

Nichiren-shū remains a community of lay followers and ordained clergy bound by the founder's injunction — unsparing of life (不惜身命), united in heart though different in body (異体同心) — and oriented always toward the head mountain at Kuonji on Mt. Minobu.

II — Head Priest

The fifth abbot of Myohokkein.

The temple is led today by Rev. Shinma Chikō, who inherited the Dharma Lamp in 2011 as the fifth-generation abbot.

Rev. Shinma Chikō

Shinma Chikō · 1963 —
新間 智孝（しんま ちこう）

Born in Wakayama City in December 1963; raised from infancy in Kobe, on the grounds of Myohokkein after being taken in by the Shinma family. Trained in the classical curriculum of Nichiren-shū at Risshō University and ordained at Hokkekyō-ji's Great Ascetic Hall (大荒行堂), which he completed twice.

"When the Great Hanshin Earthquake came, I was standing at its centre — and it remade how I understood the question, what is faith for?"
—— 平成7年　阪神・淡路大震災の経験より

After the 1995 earthquake, Rev. Shinma spent half a year at Mt. Minobu re-studying the Lotus Sūtra and the founder's writings from the beginning. He later served as a local fire-brigade volunteer, edited an earthquake-memorial volume, ran the regional Nichiren-shū newspaper bureau, and worked in the denomination's Kinki-region administration before assuming the abbacy here in 2011.
